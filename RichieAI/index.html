02 — The Insight

The research finding that
changed everything.

My quantitative data showed the what, but it didn't explain the why. After eight user interviews, a clear pattern emerged: Users didn't distrust the AI—they distrusted themselves. 

"Most people don't avoid investing because they lack money — they avoid it because they don't trust themselves to make the right call."
— Recurring theme across 6 of 8 user interviews

Every design decision after that moment was filtered through one question: Does this build trust, or erode it?

04 — Research Insights

Three findings.
One clear direction.

Insights drawn from analytics data, session recordings, and user interviews across varying levels of investment experience.

01 Top Finding
Users trusted AI recommendations more when they could see the reasoning — not just the outcome. Opacity killed trust faster than a bad recommendation.
02 Biggest Surprise
Users didn't want more features. They wanted fewer, better-timed moments. A single well-placed insight beat a dashboard full of charts every time.
03 Key Pivot
Onboarding wasn't a form — it was a relationship. Users who felt heard during setup were significantly more likely to trust the AI's first recommendation.

06 — Solutions

Two problems.
Two solutions.

The research surfaced two distinct failure modes. Each needed its own answer.

Problem 01

Data overload kills engagement

Redesigned the home screen around a single AI insight card — one clear, personalised message each session. Portfolio data moved one level deeper, accessible but not the first thing users see.

Data without context isn't information — it's anxiety.

Problem 02

Black-box AI breaks trust

Introduced a transparent reasoning layer — every AI recommendation shows a 2-sentence explanation of why Richie is suggesting it. Users can expand for full detail or act immediately.

7 of 8 users trusted a recommendation more when they understood the why.

A system that scales from beginner reassurance to expert depth — without switching contexts.

AI Insight Card
One personalised message per session surfaces on the home screen. Portfolio data lives one tap deeper — present but not overwhelming. The card adapts its tone based on user profile: reassuring for Alex, concise for Sarah, data-rich for James.
Transparent Reasoning
Every AI recommendation includes a visible 2-sentence explanation. Users can expand to see the full reasoning chain. This single feature increased trust scores across all three personas in usability testing.
Conversational Onboarding
Goal-setting and risk tolerance captured through a chat-like interface rather than forms. Users who completed this flow showed 2.1× higher retention — they felt heard before they were shown anything.
Proactive Alerts
Smart notification system that alerts only on meaningful portfolio events — not every price tick. Frequency tuned per user profile. Sarah gets weekly digests; James gets real-time signals on his chosen watchlist.

Reflection

Designing for trust is harder
than designing for usability.

Richie AI taught me that in fintech, trust is the product. Every interaction either builds or erodes a user's confidence. The technical capability of the AI meant nothing if users couldn't understand or rely on its decisions.
